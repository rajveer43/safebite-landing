Case history

What the dining room doesn't show you

01

The kitchen is out of sight, on purpose

A polished dining room says nothing about food handling, cold storage, or what's happening near the fryer. That's the part a menu can't show you.

02

Licenses lapse quietly

An FSSAI license or fire safety certificate can expire mid-quarter and nobody outside the kitchen finds out — until SafeBite flags it.

03

Complaints disappear into star ratings

A one-star review about pests gets buried under fifty reviews about the parking. It never reaches anyone who can actually act on it.

Procedure

How a rating gets built

Four steps, in order — the same path an inspector's report takes before it reaches your screen.

  1. 01

    Search nearby

    Restaurants around you, ranked by distance or by score.

  2. 02

    Read the score

    One number out of 100, weighted from inspections, complaints, and certificates on file.

  3. 03

    Open the file

    Every certificate, every inspection date, every complaint status — on the record, not summarized away.

  4. 04

    File a report

    Something wrong? It goes to an inspector's queue, not a comments section.
