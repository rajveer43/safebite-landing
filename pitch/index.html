The problem

You can't see the kitchen before you order.

01

The kitchen is invisible by design

A clean dining room says nothing about food handling, cold storage, or pest control — the part that actually determines whether you get sick.

02

Licenses lapse quietly

An FSSAI license or fire safety certificate can expire mid-quarter, and nobody outside the kitchen finds out — until something goes wrong.

03

Complaints disappear into star ratings

A one-star review about pests gets buried under fifty reviews about parking. It never reaches anyone who can actually act on it.

Who this hurts

Three people, one blind spot.

The diner

Guesses instead of knowing

A parent, a student, or an elderly regular picks a restaurant on habit or star rating — with no way to check its actual safety record before ordering.

The honest owner

Loses business to unsafe competitors

A restaurant that keeps its certificates current and passes inspection gets no visible credit for it — and loses customers to places that cut corners.

The inspector

Buried in paper trails

Inspection reports and complaint reviews live in disconnected paper and siloed government systems, slowing down the enforcement that's supposed to protect people.

SafeBite3 / 12

The solution

One transparent score, per restaurant.

✓

SafeBite pulls real inspection reports, certificate status (FSSAI, fire safety, health, hygiene), and filed complaints into one place.

✓

Every restaurant gets a single 0–100 safety score — weighted, auto-calculated, and visible before you order.

✓

Owners can see exactly what's pulling their score down and fix it. Inspectors log findings straight from the field.

✓

Complaints go to an inspector's queue — not a comments section that nobody official ever reads.

How it works

Four steps, in order.

The same path an inspector's report takes before it reaches your screen.

  1. 01

    Search nearby

    Restaurants around you, ranked by distance or by score.

  2. 02

    Read the score

    One number out of 100, weighted from inspections, complaints, and certificates.

  3. 03

    Open the file

    Every certificate, inspection date, and complaint status — on the record.

  4. 04

    File a report

    Something wrong? It reaches an inspector's queue, not a review void.

A challenge we overcame

Debugging backwards, then fixing it forward.

$ npx expo start
ERROR Project is incompatible with
this version of Expo Go.
// assumed: SDK too old → upgraded
ERROR still incompatible.
// read the exact error text —
// realized: phone's Expo Go was OLDER,
// not newer. Downgraded SDK instead.
✓ App running on physical device

Early on, the app kept crashing on a physical Android phone with a version-mismatch error. We assumed the SDK was outdated and upgraded — the error didn't go away.

Only after reading the exact error text did we realize we'd diagnosed it backwards, and downgraded instead.

That mistake became the rule for the rest of the build: never guess — read the exact error before acting.

Growth plan

City by city, data first.

Phase 1

Partner with one metro's food safety department

Onboard verified government inspection data first — the score's credibility depends on real records, not self-reported data.

Phase 2

Recruit local restaurant owners

Free compliance-tracking tools are a strong standalone incentive — this builds listing density ahead of a public launch.

Phase 3

Open customer-facing search

Launch to diners once a city has enough verified listings to be genuinely useful.

Phase 4

Expand to tier-2 towns

Same backend, same app — only municipal data integration needs local setup. Tier-2 towns have the least existing transparency today.
